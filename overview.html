OVERVIEW

I am currently working as a frontend designer at Yonsei University, one of the oldest private university in South Korea, established in 1885 by an American Christian Missionary. It is ranked 104th overall by the QS World University Rankings in 2019 and is one of Korea's three 'SKY' universities, a group of the most prestigious universities in the country. Admission to the university is extremely competitive, with only top 2 percent of Korean high school graduates accepted to the university each year.

Since the university has such a strong reputation in Korea, the members of the university tend to assume that the international audience will hold the institution in the same high esteem. Every PR material was based on the assumption that foreign academics and students already know us and admire us. The university offered only a basic translated version of the Korean website that did not appeal to the international audience. When it comes to social media, it does not even have a English version. Moreover, the university was hard to be found online unless you actively type in the name of the university on a search engine, which is not easy unless you are familiar with Korean. The false assumption was depriving us of a vast majority of non-Koreans who are interested in studying or researching in Korea but do not necessarily know about the university.

Most Korean universities are bureaucratic organizations that have a structure based on traditional functionality. The structure and the number of people assigned to the development and management of university websites were based on the assumption that websites are online version of brochures, built by an outside agency, whose content is updated by the PR department periodically and technical issues handled by the IT department. Thus not a single in-house UX/UI designer or content creator assigned to the job prior to my involvement. Senior management would like a website that looks modern and eye-catching, but the university was on a limited budget to outsource the launching of a whole new website, and orchestrating a digital experience of international audiences did not fall into any departmentâ€™s traditional job description.
